This article provides a comprehensive weekly digest of updates and news from the Groovy ecosystem, encompassing releases, significant announcements, technical articles, conference presentations, and community interactions across social media. The period highlighted is marked by several key developments and upcoming events.

**Major Highlights and Announcements:**
A primary focus of the digest is the **launch of the beta version of the new Groovy website**. This project is open-source, hosted on GitHub, and notably built using Groovy and Gradle. Concurrently, a bug-fix release, **Groovy 2.3.4**, was made available, addressing issues related to anonymous inner class support and compatibility between AST transformations and static compilation.

Another significant announcement detailed is **Hans Dockter's revelation of the future Gradle roadmap**. This promises "revolutionary features" focusing on enhanced performance, parallelization capabilities, advanced caching mechanisms, improved sharing functionalities, and robust tooling support for future Gradle versions.

The article also emphasizes the availability of new content from the **GR8Conf Europe** conference. The organizers have begun publishing edited videos of presentations, offering "hours worth of Groovy content" for the community. Furthermore, a reminder is issued for **GR8Conf US**, which is rapidly approaching at the end of the month.

**Software Releases:**
Beyond Groovy 2.3.4, the following software updates were announced:
*   The **third beta of Griffon 2.0.0** was released.
*   **Spring Boot 1.1.4** was also made available.

*   **Mark Perry** continued his explorations into functional programming, showcasing "Groovy applicatives."
