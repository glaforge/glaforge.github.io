The most prominent news is the **final release of Gradle 2.0**, marking a crucial milestone for the project. This new version notably includes a migration to Groovy 2.3 as its baseline. This central release is complemented by several other key updates across related platforms.

**Key Releases:**
Beyond Gradle 2.0, a significant number of other projects saw new releases:
*   **Ratpack 0.9.6** was released.
*   **Grails** had two new versions: **2.4.2 and 2.3.11**.
*   The **GrooScript Gradle plugin** reached version **0.4**, offering compatibility with Gradle 2.0 and Groovy 2.3.3.
*   The **Spring IO platform 1.0** was launched, designed to be aligned with the recent Groovy and Grails releases.
*   **Lazybones 0.7** was released.
*   **Spring Boot** saw two rapid updates, with versions **1.1.2** and **1.1.3** both becoming available.
Many of these releases, including Gradle 2.0, Grails 2.4.2, 2.3.11, Lazybones 0.7, and Spring Boot 1.1.2, were also announced as being available via GVM (Groovy enVironment Manager).
