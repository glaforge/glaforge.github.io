The article announces the immediate availability of two new releases for the Groovy programming language: Groovy 2.0.2 and Groovy 1.8.8. The Groovy development team, with great pleasure, shared this update, emphasizing that these are primarily bug fix releases. While focused on resolving issues, they also incorporate several minor improvements across various aspects of the language.

Key areas that saw enhancements and improvements in these releases include the static type checker and static compilation features, which are crucial for performance and type safety in Groovy. The GroovyScriptEngine also received attention, indicating work to improve its functionality and stability. Furthermore, significant performance improvements were made, specifically in the "invoke dynamic" support, a feature vital for modern JVM languages, as well as general performance enhancements for plain Groovy code execution. Another notable update is the upgrade of the GPars distribution included with Groovy, which now utilizes the newly released 1.0-beta-3 version.

For users and developers seeking detailed information about the specific changes and fixes in each release, direct links to the JIRA changelogs are provided: http://bit.ly/gr202 for Groovy 2.0.2 and http://bit.ly/gr188 for Groovy 1.8.8. These changelogs offer a comprehensive list of resolved issues and minor improvements. The article also informs readers that both new versions of Groovy are available for download from the official download area at http://groovy.codehaus.org/Download.

The announcement concludes by expressing gratitude to all contributors who played a role in bringing these releases to fruition. Beyond the release information, the article takes the opportunity to extend an invitation for community involvement. It highlights the dedicated contribution page at http://groovy.codehaus.org/Contributing, encouraging anyone interested in contributing to the Groovy project to visit it. Looking ahead, the Groovy team plans to propose a list of specific JIRA issues at http://bit.ly/gcontrib, designed to help new contributors get started with working on the project. Additionally, the contribution page features a table outlining wider goals and side projects, offering various avenues for those looking to contribute to the Groovy ecosystem. The team explicitly welcomes ideas and questions regarding these potential areas of contribution, affirming their eagerness to receive community help.
