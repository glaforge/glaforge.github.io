The provided article announces that Groovy won the first prize at the JAX Award, an annual contest held by JAX, which is highlighted as the most important Java conference in Germany. The contest aims to identify the most innovative and creative projects within the Java community.

From a pool of over 40 proposals, a jury selected ten nominees for the award. The competition was stiff, featuring highly regarded projects such as the Matisse GUI builder integrated into NetBeans and the Nuxeo Enterprise Content Management solution. The article emphasizes the immense honor and pleasure for the Groovy community to receive this prestigious award, especially considering the quality of competing projects and the caliber of past winners, like the Spring framework in previous years.

Dierk König, the author of the best-selling "Groovy in Action" book, accepted the award on behalf of the Groovy community. König had also presented multiple sessions on Groovy during the JAX conference. He even shared a photograph of the prize itself.

The article posits that this award serves as a definitive validation of Groovy's innovation, creativity, and growing influence within the broader Java community. It underscores Groovy's status as a successful and mature project, citing several key indicators:
*   Its recent 1.0 release.
*   The publication of its dedicated book, "Groovy in Action."
*   Active development of IDE plugin support by major IDE makers.
*   Increasing adoption by companies leveraging Groovy for writing business rules and scripting their products.
*   The emergence of dedicated news sites (like aboutgroovy.com) and feed aggregators (like groovyblogs.org).
*   The establishment of dedicated conferences, such as the Grails Exchange, and dedicated tracks at major events.
*   The significant presence of over 10 sessions focusing on Groovy and its web framework, Grails, at the upcoming JavaOne conference.

In conclusion, the author expresses gratitude to all the Groovy committers, contributors, and the entire Groovy community. The article firmly states that this award is a collective recognition of their efforts, highlighting that the community is integral to Groovy's current success and appeal.
