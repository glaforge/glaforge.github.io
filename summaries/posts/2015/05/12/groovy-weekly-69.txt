This Groovy Weekly update, penned by Guillaume Laforge, provides a comprehensive overview of the vibrant and evolving Groovy ecosystem, highlighting significant infrastructure changes, new releases, a wealth of technical content, and upcoming events.

The author begins by sharing his recent activities, which included spending a week in California for meetings and serving as a keynote speaker at JFrog’s SwampUp conference in Napa Valley. There, he revisited the topic of Groovy's release process and its historical context, a subject he previously discussed at Devoxx 2015. He also congratulated the Gradle team on the release of Gradle 2.4, specifically noting its significant improvements in build speed.

A critical announcement for the entire Groovy community revolves around the project's ongoing incubation process at Apache. This transition entails substantial infrastructure changes:
*   The **JIRA issue tracker has migrated** to Apache's platform.
*   **Groovy mailing-lists are in the process of moving**, requiring users to update their subscriptions.
*   The **Apache Git repository is now the official mainline** for the project.
*   A **new Apache Groovy GitHub mirror** has been established to facilitate pull requests from contributors.
The author urges all community members to update their bookmarks and register for the new mailing lists to stay connected and contribute effectively.

The update details several significant **software releases**:
*   **Gradle 2.4** was released, bringing notable performance enhancements.
*   **Ratpack 0.9.16** saw a new version.
*   **Groovy Document Builder v0.4** by Craig Burke was released.
*   **Grengine 1.0.3** and **Jexler 1.0.16** also received updates.

A substantial portion of the summary is dedicated to **articles** published by community members, covering a broad range of technical topics:
*   Mirosław Gołda discussed **automated tests using Geb, Spock, and Groovy**.
*   MrHaki explored **sharing data in concurrent environments with dataflow variables** in Groovy Goodness.
*   Bobby Warner detailed building **APIs with Grails 3 and OAuth 2**.
*   Ken Kousen presented on **concurrent programming using GPars**.
*   GrooScript offered insights into **client-side Groovy templating in the browser**.
*   Eric MacAdie elucidated the **@Hidden annotation in Groovy Validators**.
*   Schalk Cronjé provided guidance on **testing plugin compatibility across different Gradle versions**.
*   Ron Dahlgren shared expertise on **deploying Grails applications to Docker**.
*   Two articles from Groovy Algorithms introduced **Groovy profiling with Gprof** and **benchmarking with GBench**.
*   Patrick Double explained **managing JavaScript and CSS dependencies using Gradle and Bower**.
*   Tarun Pareek elaborated on **compiling Groovy scripts at runtime and implementing caching** with GroovyClassLoader.
*   Groovy superstar Tim Yates addressed a StackOverflow question regarding **Groovy's map vs. array performance**.

In **news**, Jakob Aae Mikkelsen's Grails Diary for week 19 was highlighted, providing ongoing updates relevant to the Grails framework.

The **presentations** section showcases a wealth of recorded talks, making valuable knowledge accessible:
*   Cédric Champeau presented at Bordeaux JUG on **Gradle's approach to convention over configuration**.
*   A fresh batch of **SpringOne2GX 2014 videos** was released, including talks on Grails plugin testing, API abstraction in Grails, combining Groovy with REST and NoSQL, single page applications with Grails and RaveJS, content-enabled web/mobile apps with Spring and Groovy, practical AST transformations, and the future of Gradle.
*   **Greach conference videos** also became available, featuring Trisha Gee on Groovy vs. Java for testing, workflow architectures with Grails, Grails and Cassandra integration, Groovy on the shell, and leveraging Groovy/Grails in Spring Boot projects.

**Tweets** from influential figures in the community provided quick updates and insights:
*   Cédric Champeau called for **developers to contribute to the Groovy Eclipse project**.
*   Dierk König highlighted that Chapter 8 of **Groovy in Action 2nd edition** offers the first complete exposition of Groovy's runtime metaprogramming capabilities and noted the book's codebase contains over 1000 power assert statements.
*   It was announced that **Griffon 2.3 will feature JavaFX specific AST transformations**.
*   **Ratpack 0.9.17** is expected to enable the use of async promises from synchronous APIs, and Robert Zakrzewski joined as a new contributor to the Ratpack project.

The multimedia content included **Groovy podcast episode 12** and a **screencast by Bertrand Goetzmann** demonstrating the creation of microservices with Grails 3.

*   Sergio del Amo's **Groovy script for interacting with WordPress' JSON REST API**.

In terms of **job opportunities**, Object Computing, the new home for Grails, announced they are actively **hiring Grails experts**.

Finally, the update reiterated the urgency to **register for GR8Conf Europe 2015** as registration was closing on May 15th, and mentioned that **speaker interviews from Greach** are available on YouTube.

In conclusion, this Groovy Weekly issue paints a picture of a highly active and evolving ecosystem. The significant move to Apache incubation marks a new chapter for the Groovy project, while continuous software releases, a wealth of educational content through articles and conference presentations, and ongoing community engagement demonstrate the health and dynamism of Groovy and its associated frameworks like Gradle and Grails.
