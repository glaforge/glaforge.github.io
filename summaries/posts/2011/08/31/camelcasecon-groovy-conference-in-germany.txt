This article serves as an announcement for the **CamelCaseCon** conference, an event specifically dedicated to the **Groovy ecosystem**.

The conference is scheduled to take place in **Düsseldorf, Germany**. The article explicitly targets individuals located in Germany, urging them not to miss this event.

CamelCaseCon's core focus revolves around the various technologies that comprise the Groovy ecosystem. Attendees can expect comprehensive coverage of:
*   The **Groovy** programming language itself.
*   **Grails**, a prominent web application framework.
*   **Gradle**, a widely used build automation tool.
*   **CodeNarc**, a static analysis tool for Groovy code.
*   **Spock**, a powerful testing and specification framework.

A significant highlight of the conference is its lineup of speakers. The event will feature presentations from a panel of renowned experts in the Groovy domain, including **Dierk König, Hans Dokter, Peter Niederwieser, Joachim Bauman, and Stefan Glase**. These experts are expected to cover all the aforementioned Groovy-related topics.

The key argument presented by the article is that attending CamelCaseCon offers the opportunity to hear the "latest news" directly from these "renowned experts," thereby providing valuable, up-to-date insights into the Groovy ecosystem. The implicit conclusion is that for anyone involved with or interested in these technologies, the conference is a crucial and beneficial event to attend.
