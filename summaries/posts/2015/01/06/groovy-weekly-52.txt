This article, a Groovy Weekly installment, serves as a comprehensive update for the Groovy community, kicking off with a festive New Year's greeting for 2015, expressing hopes for health, joy, happiness, and an abundance of Groovy code. It prominently highlights significant community growth and engagement efforts.

A key achievement underscored is the remarkable surge in Groovy's GitHub stars, which escalated from below 600 to beyond 1000 following a previous plea for community support. The author encourages continued starring and spreading the word to maintain this momentum. Additionally, Groovy Weekly itself reached a significant milestone by delivering its 2000th news item, demonstrating its consistent and long-standing contribution to keeping the community informed. Readers are reminded that they can subscribe to Groovy Weekly for direct delivery of news to their inbox and are also invited to contribute their own news items via a submission form.

The summary then details recent software releases within the Groovy ecosystem:
*   **Grooscript 1.0-rc-1:** This release candidate for Grooscript, a tool for transpiling Groovy code to JavaScript, was announced, with an emphasis on encouraging users to test it thoroughly before its final stable release.
*   **Ratpack 0.9.12:** A new version of Ratpack, a set of Java libraries for building high-performance web applications, was also released.

*   "All in Together: Android Studio, Gradle and Robolectric" by Jason Atwood, which explores the integrated use of these tools for Android development.

*   Deepak Kumar Mittal contributed the second part of a series on "Grails unique constraint optimization," offering advanced insights into improving database performance within Grails applications.

*   Jacob Aae Mikkelsen's Grails Diary for week 51 and an update on 'This month in GUM' (Groovy Users of Minnesota) were also noted.
*   An interesting discussion on the mailing list centered around the potential for "Groovy for data science" to become a "killer application" for the language, suggesting a strategic direction for its adoption and showcasing its versatility.
*   Cédric Champeau was highlighted for his ongoing work exploring ideas to improve Groovy's command-line interactions, hinting at future enhancements for developer experience and usability.
