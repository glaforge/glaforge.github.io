This Groovy Weekly newsletter issue provides a comprehensive update on the Groovy ecosystem, marking almost a year since its inception and anticipating reaching 2000 shared news items around its first anniversary. The current week is highlighted by the Groovy Grails eXchange (GGX) conference in London, indicating a strong community presence and promising future content in the form of slides and videos from the event. The newsletter also congratulates the Griffon team on their 2.1 release.

**Key Updates and Releases:**
The "Releases" section showcases a vibrant development cycle within the Groovy community, with several significant updates:
*   **Griffon 2.1.0** was released, a notable achievement for the desktop application framework.
*   **GrooScript 0.6.3** was unveiled, marking the equivalent of its first release candidate for version 1.0, indicating progress towards a stable Groovy-to-JavaScript compiler.
*   The integrated development environments saw updates with **Spring Tool Suite and Groovy/Grails Tool Suite (STS/GGTS) 3.6.3** being released.
*   Craig Burke provided new versions of his **Grails and Angular.JS Gradle asset pipeline plugins**, enhancing front-end development workflows.
*   A major milestone was reached with the **Groovy-based Jenkins Workflow plugin reaching 1.0**, signifying its readiness for production use in continuous integration environments.
*   Further GrooScript ecosystem development was seen with **GrooScript Gradle plugin v0.8**.
*   The **Asciidoctor Gradle plugin** received an update to **v1.5.2**.
*   **Groovy VFS (Virtual File System) 1.0 beta 1** was released, offering new capabilities for file system abstraction.
*   A **Windows installer for Groovy 2.3.8** became available, simplifying installation for Windows users.
*   Finally, **Gretty 1.1.8** was released, an embedded servlet container plugin for Gradle.
